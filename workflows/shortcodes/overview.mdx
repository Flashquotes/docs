Shortcodes are dynamic placeholders that automatically insert relevant information into your workflow emails. They help personalize your messages and ensure accurate information is always included.

Short codes are formatted as `{{entity.attribute}}` and must be surrounded by `{{}}` to be translated to a dynamic value.

<Tip>
	After adding shortcodes to a message template, [preview
	it](/workflows/templates#testing-your-templates) with a quote to make sure
	they work as expected
</Tip>

## Available Shortcodes by Trigger

Specific shortcodes are only available for certain triggers based on what data is available at that stage of the customer journey.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Quote Sent / Quote Requested">
    ### Lead Information
    | Shortcode | Description | Example |
    |-----------|-------------|---------|
    | `{{lead.first_name}}` | Lead's first name | John |
    | `{{lead.last_name}}` | Lead's last name | Doe |
    | `{{lead.full_name}}` | Lead's full name | John Doe |
    | `{{lead.email}}` | Lead's email address | john@example.com |
    | `{{lead.company_name}}` | Lead's company name | Acme Inc |
    | `{{lead.phone}}` | Lead's phone number | +1 234-567-890 |
    | `{{lead.city}}` | Lead's city | New York |
